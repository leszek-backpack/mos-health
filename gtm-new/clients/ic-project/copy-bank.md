# IC Project — Email Copy Bank

**Client:** IC Project (icproject.com)
**Language:** Polish (all email copy) | English (strategic annotations only)
**Pricing:** 39-129 PLN/user/month
**Differentiators:** EU data residency, integrated financial module, Polish-language support, industry templates
**Case Studies:** ATAL S.A. (construction), Wawel S.A. (manufacturing)
**Framework:** SPARK (Subject - Personalized Opening - Agitate Problem - Relevant Value - Kick-off CTA)
**Rules:** 60-120 words per body. One CTA per email. Earn the reply. No buzzwords. No "rozwiazanie" in subject lines.

### A/B Testing Notes

**Construction Sequence:**

| Step | Variant A Tests | Variant B Tests |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Step 1 | Statement-led opening, specific metrics (5+ hrs, 60%, 7 cities) | Question-led opening, conversational tone, explicit time ask (15 min) |
| Step 2 | Data-first (benchmark report offer) | Asset-first (template offer with feature list) |
| Step 3 | Direct pivot ("zmieniam kat"), CFO stakeholder expansion | Peer-validated ("temat nr 2"), direct question to prospect |
| Step 4 | Professional/formal breakup | Casual/emoji breakup, lighter tone |

**Manufacturing Sequence:**

| Step | Variant A Tests | Variant B Tests |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Step 1 | Hypothesis-led ("zgaduje, ze..."), dept-specific pain | Question-led (tool inventory), industry pattern |
| Step 2 | Bullet-point metrics (3 data points) | Single provocative question + multiplied impact |
| Step 3 | Cost framing (fragmentation expense), tool inventory | Pattern recognition ("widze wzorzec"), ERP complement positioning |
| Step 4 | Professional/formal breakup | Casual/emoji breakup, lighter tone |

**Key tests across both sequences:**
- **Tone:** Formal authority (Variant A) vs. conversational peer (Variant B)
- **Opening style:** Statement/observation vs. question
- **CTA style:** Open question vs. time-bounded micro-commitment
- **Breakup tone:** Professional distance vs. casual warmth
- **Subject lines:** Company-personalized vs. curiosity/value-forward
